Design Idea

The project uses a simple question: how far can a stool be reduced while still feeling stable and complete? A three-leg layout avoids rocking on uneven floors and keeps the footprint compact.

Instead of adding bracing low down, the design brings the structure closer to the seat, keeping the lower half visually open and lightweight. The overall aim is a stool that feels honest and calm — something that looks straightforward, but rewards closer attention through small decisions in proportion and joinery.

Form & Structure

Tri Stool is built around three tapered legs and a thick seat with softened edges. A triangular ring under the seat locks the legs into position and reduces lateral movement. This ring works as both structure and detail: it stiffens the frame while creating a clear “shoulder line” under the seat.

The leg angles are kept subtle, so the stool reads vertical and quiet, but still gains stability through spread. The seat thickness gives visual weight at the top, balancing the slenderness of the legs.

The triangular ring structure locking the legs.

Material & Finish

The design keeps the wood grain visible and relies on sanding and edge rounding rather than surface treatments to create softness.

Small variations in grain and knots are left present, giving the piece a lived-in character instead of a polished, uniform look.

Reflection

This project reinforces an interest in making-led design, where proportion, structure, and material behaviour guide the form. Tri Stool sits as a small personal continuation of earlier stool work — same material honesty, with a different structural strategy and a tighter footprint.

Tri Stool

Tri Stool is a three-legged wooden stool developed as a personal making project in Helsinki.

Built from solid timber, it focuses on stability with fewer parts, creating a form that sits comfortably in everyday spaces. The design highlights clear structure, quiet proportions, and a surface finish that lets the wood speak.
