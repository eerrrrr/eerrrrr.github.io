Design Context

The site lies within Longtan Old Street, an area with strong Jiangnan characteristics and a long cultural history. The Tea Room is positioned where the slope begins to rise more steeply.

Rather than standing as a destination object, it works as part of the larger movement system, supporting both local residents and visitors moving between the town and the mountain.

Structure & Details

The design focuses on creating a calm, inward-looking space within an active natural setting. The core idea is to use a structurally optimised system that carries loads efficiently while creating an expressive ceiling surface.

Material & Atmosphere

The interior uses repurposed wood and concrete. The material choice reinforces a tactile, warm atmosphere and ties the building to local construction practices. Wood is used not as decoration, but as a way to shape light, enclosure, and acoustic comfort.

The warm, wood-lined interior during summer.

The interior provides a cozy shelter against the winter snow.

Spatial Experience

Tea drinking requires attention and slowness. The design introduces moments where the surrounding scenery is softened or partially filtered, allowing users to focus on taste and aroma before engaging fully with the view outside.

Framed views of the greenery connecting inside and outside.

The large windows capture the serene winter landscape.

Site Strategy

The surrounding landscape includes dense vegetation, mountain terrain, and a network of historic paths that link daily village life with the hillside. The approach path is deliberately indirect, using height changes to slow movement.

Aerial view of the existing village and the forested hillside site.

Site analysis showing the historic path, topography, and building location.

Site plan integrating the new volume with the existing contours.

Reflection

The Tea Room explores how small-scale architecture can shape experience through sequence, section, and material rather than formal expression. By working closely with terrain, vegetation, and movement, the building becomes part of the hillside route rather than a detached object.

The building sitting quietly in the forested slope.

The Tea Room

The Tea Room is a small hillside building in Longtan, Nanjing, located along the main route connecting the mountain to the Cultural–Tourism Center.

The project sits on a sloped, forested site and is approached through a gradual ascent. The building is conceived as a quiet stop along this route, offering space for rest, reading, and informal gathering.
