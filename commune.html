Design Idea

The project starts from the question of how existing residential buildings can be densified without losing social quality. Rather than treating the extension as a separate object, the new volume is designed as a continuation of the existing building.

The upper level is conceived as a shared “communal living room.” Placing collective space above the housing allows daylight, views, and openness to support informal use, while keeping private apartments clearly defined below.

Concept sketch: Residential extension below, communal multifunctional space above.

Spatial Organisation

The extension is organised vertically. The lower level contains new apartments to increase capacity, while the upper level is reserved for community use—functioning as a library, lounge, or event space. Balconies act as a transitional zone, connecting the interior to the landscape.

Axonometric view showing the timber volume integrated with the existing brick structure.

Structure & Timber System

The extension uses a 5.4 m × 6 m structural grid. The main load-bearing system consists of LVL post-and-beam columns (300x300mm) and beams, with CLT panels used for floors and walls. This system allows for prefabrication, precise assembly, and a clear separation between structure and envelope.

Balcony & Facade System

The balcony features a sliding wooden panel system that functions as sun shading. Users can adjust the panels to control light and privacy. The construction is modular, designed for easy assembly on site using prefabricated components.

Prefabrication & Assembly

The entire extension is designed for prefabrication. The sequence moves from the structural frame to the envelope, and finally to the interior finishes, ensuring a clean and efficient construction process.

Step-by-step assembly sequence: from structural frame to final envelope.

Reflection

Commune demonstrates how timber extensions can contribute not only additional area but also social value to existing buildings. By balancing spatial quality with structural clarity, the project explores how coordination between programme, structure, and envelope can support sustainability and everyday use.

Commune

Commune is a timber extension proposal for an existing multi-storey residential building at Katajaharjuntie 22 in Helsinki.

The project investigates how an added wooden volume can extend the life of an existing structure while introducing shared spaces that support everyday community use. The extension combines new housing with a flexible communal floor, forming a clear relationship between private and collective living.
